What Is CSM?

Jan 21st, 2013

CSM is a Stop Motion web-based system. It’s free and opensource software, using technologies like Ruby, Sinatra, Coffee-Script and FFMpeg.

CSM was born from a frustration… A while ago, I worked developing and porting a stop-motion software. The job of porting was interesting, but I have faced so many problems that it caused me to think of an easier way to have an application that depends on Hadware across platforms without bothering to write code to access the cameras directly. After that, I started an internship and met a guy who is a really good web developer. I talked to him about the ideia of a stop motion system embedded in a browser and he liked. I started looking for a way to grab frames from a camera to my browser. I found some Flash softwares to do that, but I didn’t want something that were not opensource, and then I found HTML5!!! HTML5 had the ability to access cameras, microphones and other multimedia stuff. I found some examples and I tried to implement them, but I screwed up (I’m a terrible web developer). Then I gave the task to Lucas (the guy I’ve met at the company) and in 2 or 3 days, he had some preview of the camera working inside the browser.
